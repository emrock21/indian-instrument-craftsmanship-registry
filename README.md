# Indian Instrument Craftsmanship Registry

This registry documents the regional craftsmanship traditions behind India’s classical musical instruments.  
Each entry records the lineage, materials, techniques, and acoustic identity of a specific instrument-making school.

## Purpose

To preserve India’s lutherie heritage on-chain in a permanent, verifiable, and censorship-resistant format.

Each entry includes:

- region  
- lineage or workshop  
- instrument name  
- materials  
- crafting technique  
- acoustic traits  
- cultural uniqueness  

## Why this registry matters

India’s classical instruments are built by highly specialized lineages, including:

- Miraj Sitar Makers (Maharashtra)  
- Delhi & Lucknow Tabla Makers  
- Tanjore Veena Workshops (Tamil Nadu)  
- Rajasthan Sarangi Makers  
- Palakkad Mridangam Makers (Kerala)  
- Varanasi Shehnai Makers  
- Thanjavur Nadaswaram Artisans  
- Kashmiri Santoor Craftsmen  

Each lineage preserves unique acoustic knowledge, materials, and construction techniques.
